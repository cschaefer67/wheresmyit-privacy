Privacy Policy for Where's My _it?

Effective date: June 9, 2026

Summary

Where's My _it? is designed to respect your privacy. Almost everything you create in the app — item names, notes, photos, and locations — stays on your device. The app has no user account, no login, no advertising, and no analytics. We do not collect, sell, or share your personal information.

The only situation where any of your data leaves your device is if you choose to enable the optional AI features. In that case, photos you ask the app to analyze are sent directly from your device to the AI provider you selected, using an API key that you supply.

Data we store on your device

When you log items, the app stores the following on your device only:

• Item names, descriptions, notes, and synonyms
• Photos of items and their locations
• Optional GPS coordinates of where an item was stored
• Optional device compass heading and tilt captured with location photos
• Place categories (Home, Office, School, Other, Luggage), rooms, containers, and trip names

This data is saved in the app's private storage on your iPhone or iPad using Apple's SwiftData framework. It never leaves your device unless you explicitly enable AI features, back up your device via iCloud or iTunes, or transfer your device to a new one.

Data we do not collect

• We do not have a server, so we do not collect anything centrally.
• We do not run analytics, telemetry, or crash reporting SDKs.
• We do not display advertising or use advertising identifiers.
• We do not share or sell your data to third parties.
• We do not track you across other apps or websites.

Permissions and how we use them

The app may request the following iOS permissions. You may grant or deny each one:

• Camera — to take photos of items, containers, and storage locations.
• Photo Library — to attach existing photos from your library to logged items.
• Location (While Using the App) — optional, used to tag an item with the GPS coordinates of where it was stored, so you can later use the compass to find it.
• Motion & Heading — optional, used to record the device's orientation when a location photo is captured, so the app can later guide you back to that direction.

All data collected via these permissions stays on your device, except when you explicitly use the AI features described below.

Optional AI features

The app includes optional AI-powered object recognition. These features are off by default. They only activate after you:

1. Configure them in AI Settings within the app
2. Choose a provider (Google Gemini, OpenAI / ChatGPT, or Anthropic Claude)
3. Enter an API key you obtained directly from that provider

When AI features are enabled and you use them, the app sends the relevant photo and a short text prompt directly from your device to the provider you selected. We do not operate any server in this process — your device communicates with the provider's servers using your API key. We never receive a copy of your photos, your API key, or the AI responses.

Each provider has its own privacy practices. Please review them before using their services with this app:

• Google Gemini — https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/terms and https://policies.google.com/privacy
• OpenAI (ChatGPT) — https://openai.com/policies/privacy-policy
• Anthropic (Claude) — https://www.anthropic.com/legal/privacy

Your API key is stored on your device only and is sent only to the matching provider's API endpoint as part of authenticating your AI requests.

Children's privacy

The app does not knowingly collect data from children. It is rated 4+ because it does not contain age-restricted content, but it is intended for general use rather than as a child-directed product.

Deleting your data

All app data lives on your device. To delete it:

• Delete a single item: swipe-to-delete in the app, or use the trash icon in the item detail view.
• Delete all data: uninstall the app. iOS will remove all of the app's local data along with it.

If you previously connected an AI provider, you may also wish to delete the API key from that provider's console.

Changes to this policy

If we update this policy, the new version will replace this page and the effective date above will change. Material changes will be noted in the app's release notes.
